Research

Knight Lab

After learning more about visual perception and a bit of Psychophysic research methods, I decided to learn more about how the brain itself reacts when presented with different types of visual stimuli. This time I looked at brain waves using EEG while I was running visual perception experiments within Matlab.

Banks Lab

Ever since that one Perception class I took at UC Berkeley, there was a curiosity ignited within me to understand what makes the brain tick when it comes to visual perception. While at Berkeley I was privileged to receive hands-on experimental design training with Matlab in signal detection theory, psychophysics, quantitative data analysis, and Bayesian to test stereoscopic vision and 3D perception.

Honors Thesis

An interdisciplinary thesis drawing from architecture, neuroscience, city planning, and mental health. This research takes a closer look into how city living affects the mind, while presenting possible implications and as well as highlighting the importance of future research.

In 2008, for the first time in history there were more people living in cities than in rural areas. This expansion is projected to increase and new growth is expected to be heavily concentrated in Africa and Asia. Along with this growing trend in urbanization, there is an urgency to focus on how these city structures are influencing and shaping the emotional health of their citizens.
