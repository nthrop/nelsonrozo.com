Building the Help Center

An Image of Our Help Center

GOING BEYOND JUST ORDINARY

We live in a digital age where self-service options online are a must. Our effort was to create the online resources that would live and breathe to assist our customers.

In order to make the most user-friendly product possible, we had to meet the customer with resources they found useful.

By creating content that was within their preferred method of consumption, we aimed to amplify our online articles, videos, and email communication to be easily accessible.

Creating Accessible Content

The first step of building content for our Help Center was to determine which mediums were most helpful. We decided on the following:

  • Video tutorials to showcase new features and provide easy-to-follow troubleshooting steps.
  • Illustrated articles that helped guide the user along with clear expectations for each step.

During this process I worked closely with both the product team and customer experience team to ensure consistency across our marketing and email communications.

Finding the weak spots

Every medium has its weaknesses. It was my job to help design for those and thoughtfully craft a solution.

Here are a few principles that I followed to address weak spots for the Help Center.

For written articles:

  • To visually guide attention to important sections of an article: margins and white space are helpful to direct attention and prevent eye fatigue
  • To prevent confusion and to aid users who are skimming content: use engaging and relevant images that make sense even when the user doesn't read the article text

For video tutorials:

  • To actively engage the user: tell them upfront what to expect from the video so they are more likely to remain interested and know whether or not it's worth their time
  • To prevent users from getting lost: have a good flow from one concept to the next, while carefully explaining each step; even if they pause the video, they shouldn't feel lost when they resume

Improving Navigation & Usability

Since there were many articles spanning topics such as features, FAQs, and troubleshooting (among others), I made it my mantra to make content easily searchable and to prevent important topics from being buried.

Information Architecture played a prominent role as I organized the site map into easily digestible chunks that even a first time user could easily navigate.
